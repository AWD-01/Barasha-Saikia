Favorite Destinations

Kaziranga National park

Kaziranga is famous for being the world's largest stronghold of the one-horned rhinoceros, which it protects in a landscape of tall elephant grass, marshes, and forests. It is also known for its healthy populations of other "Big Five" animals, including the Bengal tiger, Asian elephant, wild water buffalo, and swamp deer, and was designated a UNESCO World Heritage Site for its conservation efforts.

Mountains

Madan Kamdev

Madan Kamdev is an archaeological site in Assam, India, known for its 9th-10th century temple ruins and often called the "Khajuraho of the East" due to its explicit sculptures. The site is a significant historical and cultural landmark, featuring remnants of over 20 temples, a museum with ancient artifacts, and a unique underground temple. It is located near Guwahati and is a popular tourist destination for its historical significance and unique architectural remains.

Beach

Lakhimpur Pukhuri

Lakhimpur Pond is one of the major attractions of Darrang district. It is the largest pond in Darrang. This historic pond was excavated during the reign of Koch King Dharmanarayan, between the 16th and 17th centuries.

City

Deepor Beel

Deepor Beel is a freshwater lake located southwest of Guwahati, Assam, India, known for its biological importance and role as a storm water storage basin for the city. Designated as a Ramsar site in 2002, it is a crucial staging point for migratory birds and is a habitat for numerous bird species and other wildlife, making it a popular destination for birdwatchers and nature lovers. The location is 26°08'N 91°39'E.
